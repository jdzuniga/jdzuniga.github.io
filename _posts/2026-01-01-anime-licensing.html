In the competitive world of anime distribution, licensing decisions can make or break a streaming platform's success. The wrong bet on an underperforming title wastes budget and shelf space; the right one can drive subscriber growth for years. This project presents an interactive decision support dashboard that transforms raw anime industry data into actionable intelligence, helping content licensors evaluate both historical catalog value and future acquisition potential.

Anime licensors face a critical challenge: which titles should they invest in? With hundreds of new anime releases each year and limited budgets, making the wrong choice can result in significant financial losses. Traditional methods rely heavily on gut feeling, franchise recognition, or waiting to see what becomes popular—by which time licensing costs have skyrocketed.

The Challenges

Anime licensing sits at the intersection of cultural intuition and financial risk. Several structural problems make smart acquisition genuinely difficult:

  • With over 300 titles released annually, identifying which shows will resonate with audiences is increasingly difficult
  • Licensing fees for popular titles can range from tens of thousands to millions of dollars
  • Licensors need to predict success before a show airs
  • Genre trends shift rapidly, making historical data less reliable
  • A recognizable studio name carries a floor of credibility, but not a guarantee

The Data Foundation

The project is built on a star schema hosted in Supabase, designed to reflect the genuinely multi-dimensional nature of anime metadata.

Core tables:

  • dim_anime: anime_id, type, source material, rating, episode count, air date, status, duration
  • fact_anime: score, scored_by, score_prediction, rank, popularity, favorites, and members
  • dim_genre, dim_studio, dim_producer, dim_demographic, dim_licensor, dim_theme: normalized dimension tables
  • Bridge tables (bridge_anime_genre, bridge_anime_studio, etc.): handle many-to-many relationships, since a title can belong to multiple genres, involve multiple studios, and target multiple demographics simultaneously

The Solution

I built an interactive decision support dashboard that aggregates multiple data dimensions to provide a comprehensive view of the licensing landscape. The tool enables stakeholders to evaluate titles based on quantitative predictions rather than intuition alone.

Core Features

  • Predictive Scoring System: The dashboard displays predicted scores for upcoming titles, calculated using a machine learning model trained on historical anime performance data. This allows licensors to identify potential breakout hits early in the production cycle.
  • Genre Trend Analysis: The dashboard tracks genre popularity over time, revealing crucial patterns. The visualization shows that Action and Fantasy have maintained relatively stable popularity from 2020-2026, while Romance experienced a notable surge in 2025 before declining. These insights help licensors align their acquisitions with current audience preferences rather than past trends.
  • Studio Performance Metrics: A studio can accumulate large audiences through volume just as easily as through excellence. What it signals is the ability to build and retain audiences over time, capturing production consistency, distribution quality, and marketing relationships in a single number.
  • Audience vs. Score Scatter Plot: The scatter plot plots every title with audience size on the x-axis and community score on the y-axis.
  • Comprehensive Title Rankings: A sortable table presents all upcoming titles with their predicted scores, studios, genres, expected members, and engagement rates. Licensors can filter by studio and year range, enabling targeted analysis of specific market segments.

Key Insights

  • Using the scatter plot:
  • Genre diversification: Single-genre shows perform less reliably than those blending complementary genres (Action + Adventure + Fantasy)
  • Engagement doesn't equal quality: Some high-scoring titles have modest engagement, suggesting niche appeal versus mainstream popularity
  • Seasonal patterns: Clear cyclical trends in genre popularity suggest optimal timing for different content types

Conclusion

The anime industry generates billions in revenue annually, yet many licensing decisions still rely on limited information and industry instinct. This Anime Licensing Decision Support system demonstrates how data analytics can augment human expertise, reducing risk and improving portfolio performance. By combining predictive modeling, trend analysis, and comprehensive studio intelligence, the platform empowers licensors to make confident, data-backed decisions in an increasingly competitive market. It's not about replacing human judgment—it's about giving decision-makers the insights they need to trust their instincts.
